Information theory: Claude Shannon, 1948.

Game theory: John von Neumann and Oskar Morgenstern, 1944 (with important embellishments from John Nash in the 1950s).

Oxygen theory of combustion: Antoine Lavoisier, 1770s.

Plate tectonics: Alfred Wegener, 1912; J. Tuzo Wilson, 1960s.

Statistical mechanics: James Clerk Maxwell, Ludwig Boltzmann, J. Willard Gibbs, late 19th century.

Special relativity: Albert Einstein, 1905.

General relativity: Einstein, 1915.

Quantum theory: Max Planck, Einstein, Niels Bohr, Werner Heisenberg, Erwin Schrödinger, Max Born, Paul Dirac, 1900–1926.

Evolution by natural selection: Charles Darwin, 1859.

Heliocentrism: Copernicus, 1543.

Parakeets:-10 to 15 years—but, says Blanchette, “it definitely depends on nutrition, care, and exercise,” which is true for all pet birds.

Cockatiels:-Miniature cockatoos, weeros, quarrions.

Canaries:-Simply canaries—though there are several breeds, including the Harz Roller and the Red Factor Canary.

Conures:-Depends on the breed, but these pet parrots can live 20-plus years.

African Greys:-There are two types of greys, the Congo African grey (CAG) and the Timneh African grey (TAG).

Amazon Parrots:-There are plenty of types of Amazons, including the yellow-naped and orange-winged Amazon.

Parrotlets:-Pocket parrots. There are several species, including the Pacific parrotlet and green-rumped parrotlet.

Pionus parrots:-Red-vented parrots. There are eight species of Pionus, including the blue-headed and white-capped Pionus.

Finches:-The three most common types of finches are society, Gouldian and zebra.

Ring-necked Doves:-Just Doves—but a fun fact about these birds is that they’re closely related to pigeons.

Manali is an adventure-seeker’s and nature-lover’s delight.

Bandhavgarh offers the most thrilling wildlife safari.

Assam’s Kaziranga National Park has more than 2,000 rhinos and you can take a jeep safari or an elephant-back ride into the grasslands to spot them.

Deep in the coffee country of Coorg (Kodagu), lie the lush forests of Nagarhole National Park.

A largely untouched terrain, dotted here and there with tiny villages, go to the Lahaul-Spiti valley in Himachal Pradesh.

Punakha in Bhutan has some lovely treks as well as quirky attractions such as the fertility temple, Chimi Lhakhang.

Pokhara in Nepal offers many adventure activities – from rafting and kayaking on its many rivers to hiking the Himalayan hills.

Head to Auli in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand – with its 3 kilometre long slope and a 500 metre long ski lift, Auli can give many ski resorts a run for their money.
